What is the World Cup?

The FIFA World Cup, more simply known as the World Cup, is an international soccer competition between the world's best men's national teams. It occurs once every 4 years usually in the summer. 32 teams participate in the world's most viewed sporting event. The first World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay. Since then, there have been 21 World Cup tournaments which have been won by only 8 countries. The current world champion is France, which won its second title at the 2018 tournament in Russia.
